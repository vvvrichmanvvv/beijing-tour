Places of Prayer

The Mosques of Beijing

Islam has a thousand-year history in Beijing. These mosques welcome the family for daily prayers throughout the week.

Dongsi Mosque

مسجد دونغسي
Established 1356 CE · Yuan Dynasty

The primary mosque for the family — only 8 min by car from Waldorf Astoria. Serene Yuan Dynasty architecture, reliably open for prayer, including Jumu'ah on Friday 29 May.

Dongsi South Street, Dongcheng District

Niujie Mosque

مسجد نيوجيه
Established 996 CE · Liao Dynasty

The oldest and largest mosque in Beijing, founded over a thousand years ago. The heart of Muslim Beijing, surrounded by the city's most beloved halal restaurants and shops.

Niujie Street 88, Xicheng District

Haidian Mosque

مسجد هايديان
Established 1283 CE · Yuan Dynasty

Conveniently located near the Summer Palace · this is where the family performs Jumu'ah on Friday 29 May (Day VI). A Yuan-era mosque with an active Hui community, welcoming and well-maintained.

Haidian District, near Summer Palace
في كل مكان

Beyond these three, Beijing is home to over 70 mosques. The Mercedes V-Class is equipped with prayer mats, qibla compass, and refreshed water for wudu — the driver assists at the appropriate times each day.
